Meet the Residents

🌿

Nest Builder · Female

Sunbee

She's the architect, the interior designer, and the project manager - that's a strong working woman! Sunbee builds the entire nest herself, making up to 200 trips a day to collect spider silk, dried grass, and plant fibres. The spider silk is her superglue — it holds everything together and stretches as the chicks grow. She's at it all day, errrr day, and carrying the whole project on her tiny little wings. Absolute queen behavior. Go Sunbee Go!

Cheerleader · Guard · Male

Sunbob

Sunbob's job title is Chief Cheerleader and Guard, and he takes it very seriously - we all love a supportive king! He shows up regularly to check on Sunbee, sings a few enthusiastic cheep cheep notes, and puffs out his chest to remind the neighbourhood who's watching the perimeter. Not the one building the nest, but a 10/10 hypeman. Go support your queen and man that kingdom, Sunbob - you got this little guy.

Fun Facts about Sunbirds

🌿

01

Spider silk architect

The female builds the entire nest alone using spider silk, dried grass, bark strips, and plant fibres. The silk acts like nature's elastane - flexible, strong, and able to expand as the chicks grow.

02

No hovering, please

Unlike hummingbirds, sunbirds can't hover for long. They prefer to perch beside a flower to sip nectar.

03

100 flowers a day

A single sunbird can visit up to 100 flowers daily, making them vital pollinators - especially for plants with long, tubular blossoms that most insects can't reach.

04

Speed builders

The whole adventure unfolds fast: nest takes ~7–10 days, incubation ~14 days, then fledging in just 16–19 days. Cradle to wings in about six weeks.

05

That iridescent chest

The male's throat shimmers green-purple-blue depending on the angle of light. It's not a pigment - it's structural colour, the same physics that makes soap bubbles shimmer.

06

Urban champions

Olive-backed sunbirds have thrived in Singapore's cities, nesting on balconies, lamp posts, garden arches, and reportedly even on moving vehicles. They are not deterred by humans at all.

07

Solo incubation

The female incubates the eggs alone, leaving only briefly to feed. The male guards the perimeter and sings. Division of labour, sunbird style.

08

Asia's answer to hummingbirds

Sunbirds fill the same ecological role in Asia and Africa that hummingbirds play in the Americas - long beaks, nectar-drinking, iridescent feathers - and they evolved this way completely independently.
